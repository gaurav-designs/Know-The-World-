🇩🇿 Algeria
Capital: Algiers

Algeria, located in North Africa, is the largest country on the continent and the 10th largest in the world, covering about 2.38 million square kilometers. Its landscape features the vast Sahara Desert, the Atlas Mountains, Mediterranean coastline, and fertile northern plains. The Sahara dominates most of Algeria, influencing climate and population distribution, while northern regions are more suitable for agriculture and urban settlement. Algeria’s strategic location links Africa with Europe and the Middle East.

Algeria has a population of around 45 million people. Most live in the northern regions near the Mediterranean coast, while the Sahara is sparsely inhabited. Arabic and Berber are official languages, with French also widely used due to colonial history. The majority are Arab-Berber Muslims, shaping the country’s cultural and religious life. Algeria’s population is relatively young, contributing to urban growth, education demands, and employment challenges.

Algeria is a semi-presidential republic. The President is head of state, while the Prime Minister leads government operations. The Parliament consists of two chambers: the National People’s Assembly and the Council of the Nation. Algeria is divided into 58 provinces (wilayas). The political system emphasizes national unity, security, and economic development, though it faces challenges regarding democracy, governance reforms, and youth participation in politics.

Algeria’s economy relies heavily on hydrocarbons, with oil and natural gas accounting for the majority of exports and government revenue. It also has resources such as iron ore, phosphate, and gold. Agriculture is significant in northern regions, producing cereals, olives, and citrus. The government promotes diversification through industrial development, tourism, and renewable energy. Challenges include unemployment, inflation, and dependence on energy exports.

Algerian culture blends Arab, Berber, Islamic, French, and Mediterranean influences. Music styles like Raï, Chaabi, and Andalusian are popular, along with traditional dances and crafts. Literature and poetry hold an important place in society. Algerian cuisine includes couscous, tajines, dates, and mint tea. Festivals celebrate religious and national events, reflecting the country’s strong traditions and family-centered values.

Algeria’s climate is diverse. The northern coastal regions enjoy a Mediterranean climate with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers. The interior and Sahara desert experience extreme conditions, with very high daytime temperatures, cold nights, and little rainfall. This climate influences agriculture, water resources, and settlement patterns, making the northern plains the most habitable and productive regions.

Algeria has one of Africa’s strongest militaries, including the Army, Navy, and Air Force. It prioritizes national defense, border security, and counterterrorism. Algeria also contributes to regional peacekeeping and international cooperation. The military has modern equipment and strategic training programs, playing an important role in the country’s security and stability.
