Argentina is a federal republic with a presidential system. The President serves as both head of state and government. The legislative branch includes a bicameral Congress with the Senate and Chamber of Deputies. Argentina has 23 provinces and one autonomous city, Buenos Aires, each with its own government. The judiciary is independent, with the Supreme Court as the highest authority. Democracy ensures free elections, civil liberties, and separation of powers. Major political parties include the Justicialist Party and Radical Civic Union. Government policies focus on economic development, social welfare, education, healthcare, infrastructure, and foreign relations, ensuring national unity and stability while balancing provincial interests.
