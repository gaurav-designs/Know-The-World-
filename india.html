India

Geography

India is the 7th largest country by area, covering about 3.3 million km² in South Asia. It shares borders with Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, and Myanmar, and is surrounded by the Indian Ocean, Arabian Sea, and Bay of Bengal. The diverse terrains range from the Himalayan mountains in the north to coastal plains and deserts in the west.

People and Demographics

With over 1.4 billion people, India is the most populous country. It is ethnically and culturally diverse with numerous languages and religions. Hindi and English are the official languages. Major religions include Hinduism, Islam, Christianity, Sikhism, Buddhism, and Jainism.

Government and Politics

India is a democratic republic with a parliamentary system. It consists of 28 states and 8 union territories. New Delhi is the capital. The President is the head of state and the Prime Minister is the head of government. The Parliament has two houses: Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha.

History and Culture

India has a rich history, from the ancient Indus Valley civilization to British colonial rule and its independence in 1947. It has vibrant cultural traditions in music, dance, festivals, and cuisine. Mahatma Gandhi was a prominent leader in its independence movement.

Key Facts

  • Capital: New Delhi
  • Official Languages: Hindi, English
  • Population: Over 1.4 billion
  • Area: 3.3 million km²
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (INR)
  • Major Religions: Hinduism, Islam, Christianity, Sikhism
