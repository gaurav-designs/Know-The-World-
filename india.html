India, located in South Asia, is the seventh-largest country in the world and the second most populous. Central India is dominated by the Deccan Plateau with rich soils and mineral deposits. The western arid regions, like the Thar Desert, experience extreme temperatures and scarce rainfall. Coastal plains and southern peninsulas host tropical forests, wetlands, and fertile agricultural lands. India has over 2,900 kilometers of coastline and numerous rivers, lakes, and reservoirs that support irrigation, hydroelectric power, and trade. The diverse terrain shapes climate, agriculture, and biodiversity, influencing the livelihoods and culture of people across regions. India is a federal parliamentary democratic republic with a President as the head of state and a Prime Minister as the head of government. The country has a multi-tier governance system consisting of the central government, state governments, and local authorities. The judiciary is independent and includes the Supreme Court, high courts, and lower courts. Elections are held regularly at national, state, and local levels, ensuring representative governance. Political parties and civil society play a major role in shaping policies and public discourse. The government implements programs for social welfare, economic development, education, healthcare, and infrastructure development. Federalism allows states to manage regional affairs, while national policies promote integration and development. India‚Äôs climate is largely tropical but varies significantly due to its diverse geography. The northern Himalayas experience alpine and tundra climates with heavy snowfall in winters. Coastal regions enjoy tropical wet climate with moderate temperatures and high humidity. The southwest monsoon, occurring from June to September, brings the majority of annual rainfall, critical for agriculture and water resources. India‚Äôs climate influences agriculture, water supply, biodiversity, and urban planning. Extreme weather events such as cyclones, floods, and heatwaves are common challenges. Seasonal variations also affect festivals, cultural events, and daily life. Regional climates shape crop patterns, clothing, architecture, and settlement patterns across the country.
