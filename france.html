Introduction

France is a Western European country known for its history, culture, art, and influence on world politics. It is one of the most visited countries in the world, famous for landmarks such as the Eiffel Tower and the Louvre Museum.

Geography

France has varied landscapes, including mountains like the Alps and Pyrenees, rolling plains, rivers such as the Seine and Loire, and a long coastline along the Atlantic Ocean and Mediterranean Sea.

Population

France has a population of about 68 million (2025). The largest city and capital is Paris, known as the "City of Light." Other major cities include Lyon, Marseille, and Bordeaux.

Politics and Government

France is a unitary semi-presidential republic. The President is the head of state, and the Prime Minister is the head of government. France is a founding member of the European Union and the United Nations.

Economy

France has the worldâ€™s 7th largest economy. It is strong in industries such as aerospace, automobiles, fashion, agriculture, and tourism. France is the leading agricultural producer in Europe.

Culture

France is famous for its contributions to literature, philosophy, art, and cuisine. French fashion houses, wines, and films are globally renowned. The French language is one of the most influential in the world.

Climate

France has diverse climates. Northern France has a temperate climate, southern France enjoys a Mediterranean climate, and the mountainous areas experience cold winters with snow.
