It has diverse landscapes including tundras, taigas, mountains like the Ural and Caucasus, vast plains, and numerous rivers such as the Volga, Ob, and Lena. Russia has a population of around 146 million people, making it the ninth-most populous country. The population is unevenly distributed; western Russia contains most of the population, including cities like Moscow and St. Petersburg, while Siberia and the Far East remain sparsely populated. Ethnically, Russia is diverse with over 190 ethnic groups, including Russians, Tatars, Ukrainians, Bashkirs, Chuvash, Chechens, and others. Major religions include Russian Orthodoxy, Islam, Buddhism, and Judaism. Population trends include low birth rates, high urbanization, and an aging population. Migration, both internal and international, affects demographic balance. Population density impacts economic development, regional policies, and social services, with rural depopulation being a growing challenge.

Russia is a federal semi-presidential republic. The President is the head of state with substantial powers, and the Prime Minister leads the government. The Federal Assembly consists of two houses: the State Duma and the Federation Council. Russia has 85 federal subjects including republics, territories, and regions. The judiciary is independent with the Constitutional Court as the highest authority. Political parties include United Russia, Communist Party, Liberal Democratic Party, and others. Federal governance is centralized, while regions have varying degrees of autonomy. Policies cover economic planning, defense, foreign affairs, social welfare, and regional development. The government is responsible for managing resources, infrastructure, and public services across its vast territory.

Russia has a mixed economy with strong emphasis on natural resources. Oil, natural gas, coal, and minerals form the backbone of its export revenue. Industries include steel, machinery, chemicals, defense, aerospace, and agriculture. The service sector, including banking, IT, and retail, is growing. Russia faces challenges such as reliance on energy exports, regional disparities, and modernization of infrastructure. Trade partnerships with Europe, Asia, and CIS countries are vital. Government initiatives focus on economic diversification, energy development, and foreign investment. Economic policies aim to stabilize inflation, create jobs, modernize industry, and ensure sustainable growth across regions.

Agriculture in Russia contributes significantly to domestic food security. Key crops include wheat, barley, oats, potatoes, sugar beets, and sunflower. Livestock includes cattle, pigs, sheep, and poultry. Fertile lands like the Black Earth region are highly productive, while Siberia relies on mechanized farming. Modern agriculture uses machinery, fertilizers, and scientific methods to improve yields. Challenges include climatic extremes, soil erosion, and regional variation. Russia exports grain and other agricultural products. Government programs aim to support rural development, modern farming, food security, and sustainable agricultural practices to maintain competitiveness in global markets.

Russian culture is rich and varied, encompassing literature, music, dance, art, and traditions. Renowned writers include Tolstoy, Dostoevsky, and Pushkin. Classical music features Tchaikovsky and Rachmaninoff. Folk traditions, ballet, theatre, and painting are globally admired. Orthodox Christianity shapes religious festivals, architecture, and customs. Russian cuisine includes bread, soups, stews, and pastries, influenced by regional ingredients. Culture reflects historical influences, climate, and geography. Education, arts, and literature remain strong pillars of Russian identity. Cultural preservation and promotion continue through museums, festivals, performing arts, and international collaborations, enhancing global recognition.

Russia has a predominantly continental climate, with long, cold winters and short, warm summers. Arctic and subarctic conditions prevail in the north, while southern areas have temperate climates. Precipitation varies, with more in the west and less in Siberia. Winters bring snow, freezing temperatures, and harsh conditions. Summers allow agriculture, transport, and tourism. Climate affects agriculture, infrastructure, energy consumption, and human settlement. Challenges include climate change impacts, permafrost thawing, and extreme weather. Adaptation strategies include infrastructure planning, energy management, disaster preparedness, and environmental policies to mitigate climate effects and support sustainable living.
