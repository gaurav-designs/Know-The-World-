Bangladesh is a South Asian country bordered by India and Myanmar, with a coastline along the Bay of Bengal. It is predominantly a low-lying deltaic region with fertile plains formed by the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Meghna rivers. The landscape includes rivers, wetlands, mangrove forests such as the Sundarbans, and some hilly areas in the southeast. The geography contributes to rich biodiversity but also exposes the country to flooding and cyclones. Fertile soil and river systems make Bangladesh ideal for agriculture, supporting rice, jute, sugarcane, tea, and vegetables. Its geographic location influences trade, transportation, and climate. The river networks are vital for inland navigation and water resources, while natural hazards shape settlement and disaster management strategies.

Bangladesh has over 165 million people, making it one of the most densely populated countries in the world. Its population is largely Bengali, with minority ethnic groups in hilly regions. Dhaka, Chittagong, and Khulna are major urban centers experiencing rapid growth. The population is young, with a median age of around 27. High population density creates challenges in housing, healthcare, transportation, and resource management. The government promotes education, health, and family planning programs to improve quality of life. Social, cultural, and religious diversity shapes festivals, traditions, and community life. Urbanization and migration influence labor markets, economic development, and regional planning. Population trends play a central role in policy-making and national priorities.

Bangladesh is a parliamentary democracy with a President as the ceremonial head of state and a Prime Minister as head of government. The Jatiya Sangsad (National Parliament) is unicameral, with members elected for five-year terms. Bangladesh has 64 districts and local administrative divisions responsible for governance and development. The judiciary is independent, with the Supreme Court overseeing constitutional and civil matters. The government focuses on economic growth, education, healthcare, infrastructure, and disaster management. Political pluralism is present, with major parties including the Awami League and Bangladesh Nationalist Party. Policies aim to reduce poverty, improve social welfare, and maintain regional stability while promoting human rights and democratic processes.

Bangladesh has a rapidly growing economy with sectors including agriculture, manufacturing, and services. It is a leading exporter of ready-made garments, textiles, jute products, and seafood. Agriculture employs a large segment of the population, producing rice, jute, sugarcane, tea, and fish. The service sector, especially IT, finance, and telecommunications, drives urban growth. Remittances from overseas workers contribute significantly to GDP. Economic growth faces challenges including poverty, infrastructure gaps, and climate vulnerability. Government initiatives like Digital Bangladesh and industrial zones aim to promote technology, investment, and industrial diversification. Trade partnerships, export promotion, and sustainable development are key strategies for long-term economic stability.

Bangladesh has a rich cultural heritage influenced by Bengali traditions, Islamic history, and regional practices. Literature in Bengali includes classical poetry, novels, and contemporary works. Music ranges from traditional folk (Baul, Bhatiali) to modern popular music. Festivals include Pohela Boishakh, Eid-ul-Fitr, Durga Puja, and Christmas. Traditional dance, theater, and crafts are important cultural expressions. Bangladesh is renowned for its handicrafts, weaving, and textile arts. Religious, linguistic, and regional diversity shape cuisine, clothing, and social customs.
